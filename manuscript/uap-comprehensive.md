# UAP: Evidence, Physics, and the Limits of Speculation

## Executive Summary

This manuscript uses three non-negotiable distinctions: **credible anomaly $\neq$ verified origin**, **classified allegation $\neq$ evidence**, and **physics vocabulary $\neq$ engineering reality**.

UAP reports are real. Some witnesses are highly credible. Some incidents remain unresolved. But no public sensor data proves extreme UAP kinematics, and no verified public evidence establishes extraterrestrial technology, recovered craft, alien biologics, inertia cancellation, zero-point propulsion, warp bubbles, or operational transmedium vehicles.

AARO's FY2024 report lists 757 reports, 708 in the air domain, zero in the transmedium domain, and 444 cases that lacked sufficient data and were placed in the Active Archive. NASA's September 2023 Independent Study Team report emphasized poor calibration, missing metadata, lack of multiple measurements, and lack of baseline data as central barriers to scientific conclusions. [^AARO-HRR-2024^][^NASA-UAP-2023^]

The physics record is equally narrow. Quantum gravity, decoherence, vacuum structure, negative energy, and the measurement problem are real scientific frontiers. They do not imply a propulsion mechanism. Every speculative bridge from known equations to UAP-like performance requires at least one unsupported process.

The correct demand is therefore simple: release the raw sensor data, platform logs, radar tapes, calibration records, and reconstruction methodology. Until then, confident origin claims are premature.

# Part I: Evidence Record

## I.1 The Three Rules

1. **Credible anomaly $\neq$ verified origin.** A trained pilot or sensor operator seeing something unexplained is evidence that an observation occurred. It is not evidence of non-human technology.

2. **Classified allegation $\neq$ evidence.** A claim made inside a classified process may be important, but classification does not validate the claim. Classification is an epistemic barrier, not a proof generator.

3. **Physics vocabulary $\neq$ engineering reality.** Casimir forces, vacuum fluctuations, topological matter, or semiclassical gravity do not imply working propulsion systems.

These rules are intentionally severe because the UAP topic rewards overinterpretation. An unresolved observation is a question, not an answer.

## I.2 AARO, SAPs, and access claims

A recurring claim is that AARO is structurally barred from Special Access Program or compartmented material. That is too strong. AARO's reporting guidance says it may receive UAP-related classified information regardless of restrictive access controls, special access programs, or compartmented access programs. [^AARO-REPORTING-GUIDANCE^]

This does not prove AARO has received every relevant record. Bureaucratic friction, compartmentalization, institutional resistance, and voluntary reporting can still create gaps. The disciplined position is: AARO has legal authority to receive SAP-related UAP information; whether every relevant item has actually reached AARO is a separate empirical question.

AARO's Historical Record Report Volume 1 stated that it found no verified evidence that the U.S. government or private industry has reverse-engineered extraterrestrial technology. [^AARO-HRR-VOL1^] That does not prove every report is mundane. It does mean the public record has not crossed the threshold required for non-human-technology claims.

## I.3 Grusch and the ICIG

David Grusch's July 26, 2023 public testimony is significant because it placed serious allegations into an official public forum. He stated that he was told of crash-retrieval and reverse-engineering programs and that he filed complaints through official channels. [^HOUSE-UAP-HEARING-2023^]

The claim remains unverified in public. Grusch publicly described information from other witnesses; he did not publicly produce craft, materials, sensor data, or documentary proof. The classified transcript of his ICIG complaint has not been released, and no public ICIG conclusion has validated the existence of non-human craft, biologics, or recovered technology. [^GRUSCH-40-WITNESSES^]

The honest assessment is: serious whistleblower allegation, legitimate oversight question, insufficient public evidence for the extraordinary conclusion.

## I.4 Aguadilla / Puerto Rico Object

AARO identifies the Puerto Rico Object / Aguadilla case as occurring on April 26, 2013. Some third-party treatments use April 25, likely because of timestamp or source-material differences; this manuscript follows AARO's official date unless explicitly discussing that discrepancy.

AARO's March 2025 case resolution assessed the objects at approximately 656 ft altitude and about 8 mph, with high confidence that they did not show anomalous performance and moderate confidence that they were sky lanterns. [^AARO-PUERTO-RICO-2025^] AARO attributed the apparent speed to parallax, the apparent split to two objects becoming resolved, and the apparent water entry to thermal crossover.

SCU reached a different conclusion and raised technical objections. That disagreement matters, but it does not prove exotic behavior. The lesson is that raw sensor data, platform logs, camera metadata, and reproducible reconstruction methodology are essential. [^SCU-AGUADILLA^][^AGUADILLA-ANALYSIS^]

## I.5 Go Fast

AARO's “Go Fast” case resolution is dated February 6, 2025, with event date January 2015. AARO assessed the object at approximately 13,000 feet, with speed in the range of 5–92 mph after wind compensation, and with high confidence that it did not demonstrate anomalous performance. [^AARO-GOFAST-2025^]

The apparent high speed is attributable to motion parallax: the aircraft and sensor platform were moving quickly relative to the background, making a slower object appear to skim rapidly over the ocean. The original complete metadata were not available, so the case is still constrained by data quality. The public record does not support extraordinary kinematics in this case.

## I.6 Nimitz

The November 14, 2004 Nimitz incident remains one of the strongest public UAP cases because it combines trained witnesses, radar reports, visual observation, and infrared video. [^NIMITZ-2004^] It should not be dismissed casually.

It also should not be inflated. The complete raw radar data, targeting-pod metadata, platform logs, and calibration context have not been released publicly. Witness credibility strengthens the case that something unusual was observed; it does not establish non-human technology or new physics. The accurate label is unresolved with incomplete public data.

# Part II: Physics Boundary

## II.1 Real gaps do not imply mechanisms

The deepest boundary relevant to speculative UAP claims is not zero-point energy or vacuum magic. It is the quantum-to-classical transition: how stable classical records and definite macroscopic objects arise from quantum dynamics. Decoherence is a real, calculable process, but it does not provide a vehicle-scale coherence mechanism.

For macroscopic objects in ordinary environments, decoherence estimates can be catastrophically large. The often-cited ~$10^{30} \text{ s}^{-1}$ scale is order-of-magnitude and depends on separation, environment, temperature, geometry, and the degree of freedom being coherently separated. The exact number is not the point. The point is that ordinary environments destroy local macroscopic coherence far faster than any known recovery scheme can handle. [^JOOS-ZEH-1985^]

## II.2 Experimental frontier

Laboratory quantum coherence has advanced, but the scale gap remains enormous. ETH Zurich demonstrated a 16.2-microgram mechanical quantum object. Pedalino et al. reported sodium nanoparticle matter-wave interferometry with masses above 170,000 Da and macroscopicity $\mu = 15.5$. [^PATEREAU-2023^][^FEIN-2019-PEDALINO-2026^]

These are important experiments. They are not evidence of vehicle-scale coherence, inertia cancellation, or spacetime engineering.

## II.3 Vacuum and negative energy

The quantum vacuum has structure. Casimir forces, Lamb shifts, and Schwinger pair-production physics are real. But the vacuum is not a demonstrated battery, reaction mass, or controllable metric medium. [^CASIMIR-1948^][^SCHWINGER-1951^]

Warp-like metrics and traversable-wormhole concepts require negative energy. Known negative-energy effects are bounded by quantum inequalities. Pfenning and Ford's analysis of Alcubierre-type metrics showed that practical warp-drive claims fail under standard QFT constraints. [^PFENNING-FORD-1997^][^FORD-ROMAN-2013^]

## II.4 Quantum gravity

Quantum gravity is a real open problem. BridgeQG, COST Action CA23130, coordinates tabletop efforts to connect high- and low-energy approaches to quantum gravity. [^BRIDGEQG-CA23130^] Gravitationally induced entanglement experiments may test whether gravity can mediate entanglement between laboratory masses. [^MARLETTO-VEDRAL-2017^]

This is foundational physics, not engineering. It does not imply metric control, inertia cancellation, or craft propulsion.

# Part III: Conditional Speculative Framework

This section is conditional only. It is not a claim that UAPs use this physics.

If independently verified public data ever established extreme performance beyond aircraft, drones, balloons, plasmas, atmospheric effects, electronic warfare, and sensor artifacts, then new physics or unknown engineering would become a serious possibility. The public record has not reached that threshold.

A speculative model would need at least five unsupported capabilities:

1. Vehicle-scale quantum coherence in air or water.
2. A mechanism for controllable inertial response.
3. Suppression of drag, shock, heating, and cavitation.
4. Controllable stress-energy sufficient to produce useful metric perturbations.
5. A compact power source and control architecture beyond known physics.

A toy inertia parameterization can expose the missing mechanism:

$$m_i(\alpha)=m_0(1-\alpha), \quad 0 \leq \alpha \leq 1$$

This is not a law. It is a placeholder. No accepted theory or experiment permits controllable $\alpha > 0$.

A punitive dimensional sanity check for active decoherence suppression is:

$$P(\alpha) \sim \alpha^2 \frac{m_0c^2}{\tau_{\text{dec}}}$$

This is not a derived propulsion power law. It is a warning about scale. For vehicle masses and microscopic decoherence times, the control burden becomes absurdly beyond known engineering.

# Part IV: Recent Public Releases and Freshness

As of this revision, no reviewed public release identified in this repository alters the manuscript's core thesis. AARO has released additional case-resolution material, including the Puerto Rico Object / Aguadilla case and “Go Fast,” but those releases weaken rather than strengthen exotic-performance claims. [^AARO-PUERTO-RICO-2025^][^AARO-GOFAST-2025^]

No resolved public case has yielded verified evidence of extraterrestrial technology, non-human biologics, or exotic propulsion. Congressional oversight continues, but no hearing has produced primary physical evidence or independently reproducible sensor reconstruction in unclassified form.

---

# Part V: Known Limitations

1. **Temporal scope.** Case counts, report dates, and public-release status can change. Claims should be rechecked before publication.

2. **Public-record boundary.** This manuscript does not assess classified evidence directly. It assesses what can be publicly verified.

3. **Speculative framework.** The conditional physics section is not a model with predictive power. It is a map of missing requirements.

4. **Citation fragility.** Footnote keys are checked by script, but full bibliographic authority still lives in duplicated Markdown references. A future version should use a single citation database.

5. **Archive files.** Files in `archive/` are non-canonical and should not be cited.

---

# Conclusion

There is no known public mechanism for UAP-like extreme performance. The public record contains credible reports and unresolved cases. It does not contain enough data to justify alien-origin claims, recovered-craft claims, or speculative propulsion claims.

The strongest position is not belief and not dismissal. It is disciplined uncertainty backed by a demand for raw data.

**Demand the evidence. Reject premature conclusions until the data can bear the weight.**
