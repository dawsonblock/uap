# Appendix A: Evidence Cases

> This appendix provides standalone case summaries and evidentiary analysis referenced by the main manuscript. For the full narrative context, see [manuscript/uap-comprehensive.md](../manuscript/uap-comprehensive.md).

## The Standard

A credible anomaly is not a verified origin. A classified allegation is not evidence. Physics vocabulary is not engineering reality. These three distinctions are the foundation of any honest assessment of the UAP record. Every claim must be tested against them. Every paragraph that violates them must be cut.

## What Is Real

UAP reports exist. Some witnesses are credible. Some cases remain genuinely unresolved after initial analysis. Over-classification is a documented problem in the U.S. defense and intelligence apparatus, and Congress has legitimate oversight reasons to demand transparency. The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) and NASA's Independent Study Team have both acknowledged that poor data quality and classification barriers impede analysis. [^AARO-HRR-2024^][^NASA-UAP-2023^] These are facts.

## What Is Unsupported

The following claims remain unsupported by any evidence in the public record:

- Recovered non-human craft with verified extraterrestrial origin.
- Magnesium-26 isotopic fragments proving alien technology.
- Black-program zero-point-energy drives in operational use.
- Reverse-Casimir refrigerators extracting usable work from vacuum fluctuations.
- Topological quantum computers recovered from 1969 crash retrievals.
- Underwater alien bases monitored by the U.S. Navy.
- AARO as a "total controlled opposition" entity designed to conceal a hidden program.

None of these claims have been validated by peer-reviewed scientific publication, unclassified government documentation, or independently reproducible sensor data. They circulate as narratives, not findings.

## AARO and Special Access Programs

A common claim in UAP conspiracy discourse is that AARO's mandate structurally excludes Special Access Programs (SAPs) and waived unacknowledged SAPs (USAPs), thereby guaranteeing that any hidden crash-retrieval or reverse-engineering program would be invisible to it. This claim must be heavily qualified.

AARO's own reporting guidance states that, by law, it may receive UAP-related classified information regardless of restrictive access controls, special access programs, or compartmented access programs. [^AARO-REPORTING-GUIDANCE^] This does not prove that AARO has actually received every relevant piece of information. It does not mean that institutional resistance to sharing SAP material is trivial. But it undercuts the categorical claim that SAPs are automatically and permanently outside AARO's reach. The honest position is: AARO has legal authority to access SAP material; whether it has exercised that authority successfully for every relevant program is unknown.

## Grusch and the ICIG

David Grusch's public statements assert that he was informed of a crash-retrieval and reverse-engineering program and was denied access to further read-ons. [^HOUSE-UAP-HEARING-2023^] The public record shows that Grusch declined to provide information to AARO despite the office's statutory authority to receive it. [^AARO-GRUSCH-FOIA^] This is a whistleblower claim about what Grusch was told, not public proof of craft, bodies, or materials. The public record does not show that the Department of Defense Inspector General or the Intelligence Community Inspector General independently validated the existence of non-human craft or biologics. What the ICIG found—if anything beyond the validation of Grusch's reasonable belief—has not been publicly released. A classified complaint is not a scientific finding. It is a procedural event that may or may not correspond to physical reality.

## Aguadilla

In March 2025, AARO released a report on the Puerto Rico incidents, including the 2013 Aguadilla video. AARO lists the date as April 26, 2013; some third-party analyses use April 25, likely because of timestamp or source-material differences. AARO concluded that the objects did not exhibit anomalous behavior or transmedium capability, that the video shows two objects rather than one splitting in two, and that AARO has moderate confidence they were sky lanterns. [^AARO-PUERTO-RICO-2025^] The Scientific Coalition for UAP Studies (SCU) has submitted technical questions to AARO and was awaiting fuller answers at the time of writing. [^SCU-AGUADILLA^][^AGUADILLA-ANALYSIS^] SCU's critique of AARO's methodology is a legitimate scientific disagreement. It is not, by itself, a refutation of AARO's conclusion or proof of exotic behavior. The honest position is: AARO's interpretation is one resolution; SCU's questions are valid; neither constitutes proof of non-human technology.

## GoFast

AARO's case resolution for the 2015 "GoFast" video attributes the apparent high speed to motion parallax [^AARO-GOFAST-2025^]—the difference in apparent motion of a slow object near the camera versus distant background. According to AARO, the object did not show anomalous performance when triangulated against available data. The honest position is not that the object is definitively a balloon or bird; it is that the public record does not support the claim of extraordinary kinematics in this case.

## Nimitz

The 2004 Nimitz incident involving USS *Princeton* and F/A-18F pilots is among the most documented UAP cases. [^NIMITZ-2004^] The right position is not "debunked." It is: credible witnesses reported something they could not identify; the public sensor data is incomplete; performance claims remain unresolved because the full radar tapes, targeting pod metadata, and platform logs have not been released; and none of this justifies a leap to non-human physics. The Nimitz case is a data problem, not a proof problem. Until the raw sensor data and reconstruction methodology are public, any claim that it demonstrates extraterrestrial technology is unsupported.

## The Physics Abuse

Conspiracy narratives surrounding UAP are not merely speculative; they abuse real physics terminology. Casimir forces, Einstein-Cartan torsion, polarizable vacuum ideas, and topological quantum computing are all legitimate topics in theoretical and applied physics. But none of them have publicly demonstrated useful gravity control, inertial mass reduction, zero-point energy extraction, or transmedium craft propulsion. Using the vocabulary of quantum field theory to describe imagined propulsion systems lends false credibility to unsupported claims. NASA's September 2023 report stated: "We find no evidence to suggest that UAP are extraterrestrial in origin," and emphasized that analysis of UAP data is "hampered by poor sensor calibration, the lack of multiple measurements, the lack of sensor metadata, and the lack of baseline data." [^NASA-UAP-GOFAST^] The physics terms are real. The engineering applications attributed to them are not.

## Classification

Classification is a real and powerful tool. It explains why certain sensor data, program budgets, and witness testimonies remain hidden. It does not, however, automatically validate the content of classified claims. The fact that something is secret does not make it true. The fact that a program is hidden does not mean it contains non-human technology. Classification is an epistemic barrier, not a proof generator. The honest analyst treats classified allegations as unverified claims, not as evidence whose mere existence confirms extraordinary conclusions.

## AARO FY2024 Statistics

AARO's FY2024 Annual Report (covering May 2023–June 2024) states: 757 reports received, 708 in the air domain, zero in the transmedium domain; 444 cases lacked sufficient data and were placed in the Active Archive; no verified evidence of extraterrestrial beings, activity, or technology. [^AARO-HRR-VOL1^] AARO's Director acknowledged in November 2024 that some cases are "true anomalies" he does not understand — but this is an admission of insufficient data, not confirmation of exotic physics.
